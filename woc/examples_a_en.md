# Appendix A

## 1. Examples of When Goals Are Counted or Not Counted After a Disconnect

1.1 If a player from Team A is on a clear breakaway or takes a penalty shot **before** a disconnect occurs (from either team), and no defenders are pressuring the attacker, and then a player from Team A or Team B disconnects **after** the attack begins, and the goal is scored — the goal **will be counted**.  
The disconnect did not affect the play.

1.2 If a player from Team B disconnects **during** an attack by Team B, and the teammates continue playing while Team A stops moving — the goal **will NOT be counted**.

1.3 If a player from Team B disconnects **during** an attack by Team B, and the teammates stop moving and start leaving the game, but a player from Team A steals the puck and scores — the goal **will NOT be counted**.

1.4 If a player from Team B disconnects **while defending**, and the teammates stop moving and start leaving the game, and a player from Team A steals the puck and scores — the goal **will NOT be counted**.

1.5 If a player from Team B disconnects, but both teams continue to play (attack and defense), **no goals or assists scored during this time will be counted**.  
The rematch will continue not from the moment of the disconnect, but from when the match actually ends.  
To avoid this, teams are advised to exit the match as soon as the disconnect occurs — do not waste time.

1.6 There may be many different situations, but these are the most common ones.  
For other cases, teams should **stream their matches and save the video recordings** for review.  
If there is no recording, the Administration will not be able to judge the situation, and the match must be **replayed** from the beginning.
